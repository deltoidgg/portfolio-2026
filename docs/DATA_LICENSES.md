# Data sources and attribution

The repository commits compact, processed artifacts so the published analysis and browser explorers
remain reproducible. Source data keeps its original licence and attribution.

## United States

The US artifact is derived from the US General Services Administration Site Scanning programme.
The analysis uses public federal website scan results and US Web Design System adoption signals.
See the provenance and freeze details in the paper preregistration and data-freeze record.

## United Kingdom

The UK artifact is derived from public website registers and a homepage scan conducted for Paper 01.
Domain inputs include GOV.UK, mySociety council data, NHS trust lists, and curated devolved-government
sources. Individual input notes and source URLs live under `data/inputs/uk-domains`.

## Licence boundary

The MIT software licence and CC BY 4.0 research-content licence do not override source-data terms.
Consumers must review the original provider terms before redistributing a source or reconstructed
dataset. The processed artifacts are published for verification and research reproducibility, not as
a substitute for the authoritative registers.
